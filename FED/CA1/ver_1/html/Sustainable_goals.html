Reduce Carbon Footprint


There are numerous strategies to lower your carbon footprint, which is the amount of CO2
and other greenhouse gases generated into the atmosphere as a result of human activity.
Here are some effective strategies to consider:


Switch it Off

 Turn off the lights when natural light is sufficient and when you leave the room. It’s that simple!


Climate Control

 Keep your temperature system on a moderate setting while you’re in the room.


Wasteful Windows

 Use your windows wisely! If your climate control system is on, shut them…if you need a little fresh air, turn off the heat or AC.


Recycle

 Recycle aluminum cans, plastics, glass, office paper, newspaper, cardboard. Pickup of office electronics, bulk metal and surplus furniture can be arranged by contacting Facilities Work Management.

What is Carbon Footprint?


A carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gases (including carbon dioxide and methane) that are generated by our actions.

What is 'Reduce Waste'?


Reducing waste is an important sustainable goal since it aids in the conservation of natural resources, the reduction of greenhouse gas emissions, and the reduction of the environmental effect of human consumption habits.

Reduce Waste


 The easiest way to reduce waste is to practice the 3Rs: Reduce, Reuse, and Recycle.


Use a reusable bottle/cup for beverages on-the-go

 You might already have a reusable water bottle, but do you use it all the time? You can put that reusable bottle to use, save money and reduce waste. By taking your own water with you, you’ll also reduce your chances of purchasing more expensive beverages on-the-go.


Use reusable grocery bags, and not just for groceries

 Just like a reusable water bottle, you may already have a reusable grocery bag, though it’s often forgotten at home. Try writing BAGS on the top of your grocery list to help you remember, or keep them in the back seat where they aren’t as easy to forget.


Purchase wisely and recycle

 You can reduce the amount of waste you produce by purchasing products that come with less packaging and/or come in packaging that can be recycled.


Curb your use of paper: mail, receipts, magazines

 In today's digital age, most businesses send bills through email, and some even give incentives to do so. More retailers are also giving e-receipts, which are useful because they are less likely to be misplaced if you need to return something. Consider purchasing digital subscriptions to your favorite magazines to read on your tablet or computer. Digital subscriptions are frequently less expensive than hard-copy versions.

Support sustainable products and practices


Supporting sustainable products and practices is crucial for promoting a healthier planet and a more sustainable future. Here are some ways you can actively support sustainability:


Choose eco-friendly products

 Look for products that are labeled as environmentally friendly, sustainable, or certified by recognized eco-labels.


Conserve energy

 Reduce your energy consumption by using energy-efficient appliances, turning off lights when not in use, and using natural light and ventilation whenever possible.


Practice water conservation

 Conserve water by fixing leaks, using water-saving devices like low-flow showerheads and faucets, and being mindful of water usage in daily activities like washing dishes and doing laundry.


Choose sustainable transportation

 Opt for public transportation, carpooling, biking, or walking whenever possible.

What does "Support sustainable products and practices" means?


Supporting sustainable products and practices refers to making conscious choices and taking actions that promote environmental, social, and economic sustainability. It involves aligning your purchasing decisions and lifestyle with principles that minimize negative impacts on the planet and contribute to long-term well-being.
